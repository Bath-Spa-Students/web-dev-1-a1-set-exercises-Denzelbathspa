FRIED RICE RECIPE

Fried rice, a beloved dish across Asian cultures, combines pre-cooked rice with eggs, vegetables, seafood, or meat. Saut√©ed in a wok or frying pan, it absorbs flavors to create a satisfying medley enjoyed alone or as a side dish. Here are the Ingredients:

Rice
Eggs
Garlic
Oyster Sauce
Luncheon Meat
Onion

INGREDIENTS:

  • Rice: Rice is a staple food in many cultures, particularly in Asia. It is a cereal grain that is the most widely consumed staple food for a large part of the world's human population.
  • Eggs: Eggs are a versatile and nutritious food. They are a good source of high-quality protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Garlic: Garlic is a species in the onion genus, Allium. It is native to Central Asia and has been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes for thousands of years.
  • Oyster Sauce: Oyster sauce is a thick, brown sauce made from oysters, soy sauce, and other ingredients. It adds a rich, savory flavor to dishes.
  • Luncheon Meat: Luncheon meat, also known as canned meat or lunch meat, is a canned precooked meat product. It is often used as a convenient ingredient in various dishes.
  • Onion: Onions are a vegetable that is widely used in cooking due to their distinct flavor and aroma. They come in various colors and varieties.

METHOD:

  1. Start by cooking the rice according to package instructions. For best results, use leftover rice that has been refrigerated for at least a few hours.
  2. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of oil and swirl to coat the pan.
  3. Add minced garlic to the pan and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  4. Push the garlic to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are fully cooked.
  5. Add diced onions and any other desired vegetables to the pan. Stir-fry until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry the rice with the vegetables until heated through.
  7. Drizzle oyster sauce over the rice and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. Finally, add diced luncheon meat or any other desired protein to the pan. Cook until heated through.
  9. Remove the pan from heat and serve the fried rice hot, garnished with chopped green onions or cilantro if desired. Enjoy!
